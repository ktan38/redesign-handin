Responsive Redesign

IMDb is a popular online website that acts as an online database of information related to films, TV shows, and other video content. These includes cast, production crew, plot summaries, ratings/reviews, and news related to the content. In this project, I analyzed IMDb’s movie page, which is the page that shows information for one specific movie.

I evaluated the usability of the current design of the movie page using three criteria: Learnability, Memorability, and Efficiency. I then created both lo-fi and hi-fi prototypes of a redesigned version of the movie page, that addresses the usability issues I identified. Finally, I re-created the page with my new design using HTML and CSS. The responsive page is redesigned for desktop, mobile, and tablet views.


Part 1: Preparation and Interface Selection

Before identifying usability problems with IMDb’s movie page, I first wanted to survey my friends to get a sense of what parts of the page are typically used. I did not get the chance to survey a wide range of people; but I did my best to get some sort of understanding of what people tend to go to IMDb for. Here are the most common use cases I identified:

  1. Learn more about the cast
  2. Read plot summary or synopsis
  3. Read reviews and scan ratings
  4. Learn about box office and budget stats
  5. Read news related to favorite and recently watched movies

Next, we’ll take a look at the page and identify usability problems. The page I analyzed is linked below. Since the page was very long (lots of scrolling), I only included screenshots related to the common use cases I identified, since screenshooting everything would take up a lot of space.

Below are the usability problems I identified, categorized by the criteria that I evaluated the page on:



Usability Problems

# Issue Type(s) Issue
1 Efficiency, Memorability Four of the five common use cases identified (1-4) are located in the same column, even though the webpage uses a two-column layout. Many of the common uses cannot be found near the top of the page, wihch is occupied by ads and links to unrelated articles. A lot of scrolling is required to find the content desired.
2 Learnability, Memorability There is a lack of hierarchy in terms of font. Most text, whether they are headers, buttons, categories, or content, are very similar in terms of font size. Headers are harder to find, and thus also harder to recall for casual users as well.
3 Learnability There is a lack of color contrast on the header, which hinders readability.
4 Efficiency, Learnability Related photos and videos are shown in rows, with very unappealing, pixelated photos lined up as sort of a "preview". These sections occupy a lot of space that forces the user to scroll more to get to desired parts of the website, and the preview photos definitely don't inform the users of anything. Thihs is confusing for first time users, and cumbersome for experienced users.
5 Efficiency There is an unnecessary amount of preview for the list of cast members, with 15 cast members listed in a table. This takes up lots of space and further adds to the scrolling issue. Also, cast members are listed in credits order, which is probably not what users typically want to see as part of a preview.
6 Efficiency The User Review section lists one full review. This might not be the best use of space, since if users want to read full reviews, they can go to the specific page for that.
7 Learnability, Memorability First-time or casual users may not know that the Plot Summary is spoiler free, and that the Full Synopsis contains spoilers.

Accessibility

I used WebAIM WAVE to test for any potential accessibility issues in the original website. There were two reoccuring themes. First, there were many places where there was very low contrast between text colors and background colors, which raises clear accessibility issues. Second, a lot of images didn't contain alternative text, which could make it hard for people who can't load images to interpret the website.

I also used a screen reader to test for accessibility issues. Images that didn't have alternative text were obviously not read, which was a problem for a website which heavily relies on images to communicate content.

The following are some observations I had about this redesign:

  • It's harder to control layout when the dimensions in the in-between regions of mobile, tablet, desktop
  • Changing the font size initially brought out some parts of the website that weren't responsive
  • After learning flex displays, it became a lot easier to organize elements while ensuring they were responsive

Afterthoughts

In the future, I think it will be more natural for me to think about usability issues when coming across different websites.
